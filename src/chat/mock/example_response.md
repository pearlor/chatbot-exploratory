## Homemade Egg Tarts

- **Time:** 90 Minutes
- **Difficulty:** Medium
- **Serves:** 8

### [Ingredients] The Canvas

* 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
* 1/4 cup powdered sugar
* 1 large egg yolk
* 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
* 1/3 cup granulated sugar
* 1/2 cup hot water
* 2 large eggs
* 1/4 cup evaporated milk

### [Steps] The Method

1. **Prepare the pastry dough:** In a medium bowl, cream together 1/2 cup of softened unsalted butter and 1/4 cup of powdered sugar until smooth. Beat in 1 large egg yolk. Gradually stir in 1 1/4 cups of all-purpose flour until a soft dough forms.
2. **Chill the dough:** Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and place it in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. This rests the gluten and prevents the crust from shrinking while baking.
3. **Make the sweet syrup:** In a small bowl, dissolve 1/3 cup of granulated sugar in 1/2 cup of hot water. Let this syrup cool down completely to room temperature.
4. **Prepare the egg custard:** Whisk together 2 large eggs and 1/4 cup of evaporated milk in a bowl. Gently pour in the cooled sugar syrup and whisk until combined.
5. **Strain the custard:** Pour the egg mixture through a fine-mesh sieve at least twice. This is the secret to getting that perfectly smooth, glass-like finish on your tarts!
6. **Press the dough into molds:** Divide your chilled dough into 8 equal portions. Press each portion firmly and evenly into the bottoms and up the sides of your tart molds. Trim any excess dough from the rims.
7. **Fill the tart shells:** Place your lined tart molds onto a baking sheet. Carefully pour the strained egg custard into each shell, filling them about 80% of the way to the top.
8. **Bake to perfection:** Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the tarts on the lower rack for 15 to 20 minutes, then reduce the heat to 350°F (175°C) and bake for another 10 minutes until the custard is set but still slightly jiggly in the center.
9. **Cool and enjoy:** Let the tarts cool in their molds for about 10 minutes, then gently pop them out and serve them warm.

### Pros and Cons of Homemade Egg Tarts

Before you begin, here is a quick look at what to expect when making this delightful pastry:

### Pros

* + You get to enjoy them warm and fresh out of the oven, which is far superior to bakery versions that have sat on shelves.
* + You can easily adjust the sweetness of the custard to suit your personal preference.
* + It is a highly rewarding baking project that is sure to impress your friends and family.

### Cons

* - Getting the custard perfectly smooth requires careful straining and temperature control to avoid souffléing (puffing up too much).
* - You will need to purchase specific individual tart molds if you do not already own them.
* - The dough requires chilling time, meaning this is not a recipe you can rush if you want the best results.

You are going to do an amazing job with these! Take your time, enjoy the process, and happy baking! If you have any questions about the techniques, I am right here to help.

### [Ending comment] Chef's Secret

The double strain is everything — pass that custard through the sieve twice and pop any surface bubbles before baking, and your tarts will come out with a finish as smooth as glass.
